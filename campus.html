 		Hajee Mohammad Danesh Science & Technology University is the first Science and Technology University in the northern region of Bangladesh. It stands away from the urban din and bustle at a beautiful and scenic location some 13km north of Dinajpur town by the side of the intercity highway that links Dinajpur to Dhaka, the capital of Bangladesh.
Hajee Mohammad Danesh (1900-1986) was a peasant leader and politician. He was born in Sultanpur village in Dinajpur district. Having obtained his M.A. in History from Aligarh Muslim University in 1931 and B.L. degree in 1932. He died in Dhaka on 28 June 1986.
HSTU offers eight degrees under seven faculties. These are:

B.Sc.(Hons.) in Agriculture
B.Sc. Computer Science and Engineering (CSE)
B.Sc. Telecommunication and Electronic Engineering (TEE)
B.Sc.(Hons.) in Fisheries
Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM)
B.B.A.(Bachelor of Business Administration)
B.Sc. in Food & Process Engineering (FPE)
B.Sc. in Agricultural and Bio-resources Engineering (ABE).


There are 7 faculties in HSTU with 36 departments.
The university Library holds a collection of more than 30,000 volumes, including bound volumes of periodicals. It subscribes to over 50 foreign journals. A complete automation of the system is in the process. The Confined Section of the library contains rare books.
The university organizes sports and other extracurricular and recreational activities. It provides intramural and extramural programmes.
